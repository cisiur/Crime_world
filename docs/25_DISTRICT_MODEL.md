# District Model

## Purpose

This document describes how districts work as the main local units of strategy.

Districts are where economy, crime, police pressure, business activity, and control become readable to the player.

## Core idea

A district is not just a chunk of map.
It is a local simulation node with its own state, identity, and strategic value.

## District properties

Each district should have a generated set of values such as:
- wealth
- poverty
- crime level
- corruption level
- police presence
- surveillance level
- business density
- population density
- property value
- transport access
- criminal influence
- public fear
- strategic value

## District personality

Districts should feel different from one another.
A district may be:
- wealthy but heavily monitored
- poor and corrupt but unstable
- industrial and logistics-heavy
- residential and recruitment-friendly
- commercial and profitable
- politically sensitive
- crime-dense and contested

## District change over time

A district should not stay static.
It should drift based on:
- business success or collapse
- criminal control
- police pressure
- public fear
- investment
- corruption
- supply chain strength
- repeated violence

## District economy

Districts should influence:
- business profitability
- rent and property values
- labor availability
- customer demand
- logistics quality
- criminal opportunity

## District crime potential

Different districts should naturally support different criminal strategies.

Examples:
- rich districts: high-value theft, extortion, discreet laundering
- poor districts: recruitment, street crime, low-cost control
- industrial districts: transport, storage, smuggling, sabotage
- entertainment districts: drugs, protection, bribery, nightlife control
- transport districts: route control, theft, logistics disruption

## District control

A district can be influenced or controlled through:
- gangs
- businesses
- corruption
- fear
- police dominance
- political ties
- logistic control

Control should not be binary only.
A district can have partial influence, contested influence, or unstable control.

## District reactions

When the player changes a district, the district should react through:
- rising or falling profit
- changed recruitment pool
- police attention
- rival interest
- business migration
- public instability

## District importance for gameplay

Districts are the main local battlefield of the game.
They are where the player can read whether the city is safe, profitable, contested, or vulnerable.

## Design principle

If a district does not affect gameplay decisions, it is too shallow.
A good district always tells the player something useful about risk, opportunity, and control.
