# Organization Model

## Purpose

This document describes how criminal organizations are structured and how they behave as living strategic entities.

Organizations are one of the main carriers of power in CrimeWorld.

## Core idea

An organization is not just a faction list.
It is a network of people, assets, loyalties, routines, ambitions, and vulnerabilities.

## Organization properties

Each organization should have:
- name
- type
- hierarchy
- territory
- resources
- influence
- reputation
- internal stability
- risk exposure
- rivals
- allies
- strategic focus

## Organization types

Possible organization types include:
- street gang
- mafia family
- cartel
- hybrid syndicate
- business-front network
- corruption network
- mercenary crew

## Internal structure

A typical organization can include:
- boss
- underboss
- lieutenants
- capos
- trusted operators
- street crews
- accountants / fixers / logisticians
- recruits and associates

## Organization behavior

Organizations should:
- seek profit
- defend income streams
- recruit useful people
- maintain trust internally
- expand where the cost is manageable
- react to rival pressure
- hide from authorities when needed
- take over weak assets
- adapt to district conditions

## Organizational strengths

Different organizations should have different strengths.

Examples:
- gangs: mobility, intimidation, street control
- mafias: legitimacy, corruption, business access
- cartels: logistics, supply chains, scale
- hybrid groups: flexibility, mixed tools, strategic adaptability

## Organizational weaknesses

Every organization should also have weak points:
- leadership risk
- betrayal risk
- supply vulnerability
- exposure to police pressure
- overextension
- poor discipline
- conflicts between strong personalities

## Leadership and succession

Organizations should be able to survive leadership changes if they are stable enough.
Weak organizations may collapse when the boss is removed.
Stronger organizations may split, reorganize, or continue under a successor.

## Territory and influence

An organization should not only own space.
It should have influence over:
- businesses
- routes
- people
- corruption nodes
- district politics
- supply chains

## Organization growth

Growth should create both power and complexity.
The more the organization grows, the harder it becomes to keep it loyal, hidden, and efficient.

## Organization vs player

The player may lead one organization, but the game world contains many others.
Those organizations should pursue their own goals and interact with the player through competition, diplomacy, bribery, and conflict.

## Design principle

An organization is good if it changes how the city behaves.
If it only changes a number on a panel, it is too shallow.
