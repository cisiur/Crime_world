# Police and Pressure

## Purpose

The world must react to the player's actions.
This reaction should not come only from police pursuit. It should come from multiple overlapping pressure systems.

## Pressure sources

- police
- intelligence services
- political pressure
- media attention
- public fear
- rival organizations
- internal instability
- financial scrutiny

## Police model

Police should not behave as a single static bar.
They should have:
- local presence
- regional capability
- corruption level
- leadership quality
- budget
- investigation focus
- political support

## Pressure effects

Higher pressure can lead to:
- raids
- arrests
- asset seizures
- surveillance
- infiltration
- informants
- stronger patrols
- harder recruitment
- business disruption

## Corruption

Corruption reduces pressure but should never fully eliminate risk.

Corruption can be used to:
- slow investigations
- hide evidence
- reduce response time
- protect businesses
- misdirect law enforcement

## Media and public response

Visible criminal actions can increase public fear and media attention.
This may influence:
- politics
- police budgets
- competitor activity
- business value
- neighborhood stability

## Internal pressure

Large organizations should also suffer internal pressure:
- loyalty problems
- succession disputes
- betrayal
- panic after major losses
- factional splits

## Design goal

Pressure systems should make the player feel power growing and danger growing at the same time.
The bigger the empire, the more it can do, but the harder it becomes to hide and control.
