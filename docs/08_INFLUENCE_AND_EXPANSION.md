# Influence and Expansion

## Design Decision

CrimeWorld uses a hybrid influence model.

Influence exists simultaneously on three layers:
- Local
- Regional
- National / Global

Each layer affects the others but never completely replaces them.

## Local Influence

Local influence is earned through control of districts, businesses, organizations, politicians and law enforcement.

Local influence determines:
- recruitment
- local corruption
- territorial control
- business opportunities
- police response

## Regional Influence

Regional influence emerges naturally from controlling multiple cities.

It improves:
- logistics
- supply chains
- reputation
- movement of personnel
- regional political influence

## National / Global Influence

Global influence is not territory.
It represents the organization's reputation, international contacts, logistics and financial power.

Examples:
- international suppliers
- foreign criminal organizations
- offshore banking
- intelligence networks
- political leverage

## Expansion

Expansion should feel organic.

Typical progression:
Single Hideout -> District -> City -> Metropolitan Area -> Region -> Country -> International Network.

## Expansion Costs

Every expansion increases complexity.

Larger organizations require:
- more trusted leaders
- better logistics
- communication networks
- internal security
- succession planning
- anti-corruption inside the organization

## Design Goal

A player should never conquer the world simply by owning more territory.

The challenge shifts over time from acquiring assets to governing an increasingly complex criminal empire.
