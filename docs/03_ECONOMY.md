# Economy System

## Economic model

The game uses sector-based economy simulation rather than tracking every individual transaction in full detail.

## Main sectors

- transport
- retail
- hospitality
- construction
- real estate
- media
- industry
- agriculture
- finance
- entertainment
- narcotics
- smuggling
- protection rackets

## Business functions

Each business can contribute to one or more of the following:
- income
- influence
- logistics
- cover
- operational access

## Legal and illegal synergy

A business can be both a legal asset and a criminal enabler.

Examples:
- a restaurant can provide income, meetings, recruitment, and laundering,
- a transport company can provide logistics for smuggling or moving goods,
- a real estate portfolio can hide ownership and increase influence,
- a media asset can reduce public pressure and shape reputation.

## Influence model

Influence in a sector should grow from a combination of:
- market share,
- strategic importance,
- connected assets,
- geographic reach,
- political support,
- criminal leverage.

Owning many small businesses is not always better than controlling one strategic asset.

## Strategic chains

The economy should produce chains of dependency.

Example:
port -> transport -> warehouse -> retail -> local prices -> public stability

This makes expansion meaningful and creates feedback loops instead of isolated bonuses.
