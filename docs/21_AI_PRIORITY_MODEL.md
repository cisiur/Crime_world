# AI Priority Model

## Purpose

This document describes how important actors decide what to do.

The AI should not be a simple reaction system.
It should act according to priorities, goals, resources, and current pressure.

## Main AI actors

The most important AI-controlled actors are:
- criminal organizations
- police units and leadership
- district-level power holders
- business owners
- political actors
- key NPCs
- informants

## Core AI structure

A meaningful AI actor should have:
- goals
- priorities
- resources
- threats
- opportunities
- relationships
- territory or domain
- risk tolerance
- current tasks

## General decision flow

1. evaluate current situation
2. rank urgent problems
3. compare opportunities
4. choose a likely action
5. execute or delegate the action
6. update the actor's state after results

## Criminal organization AI

A criminal organization should care about:
- profit
- territory
- safety
- loyalty
- retaliation
- visibility
- logistics
- recruitment
- rivals
- corruption

Possible priorities:
- protect income
- expand territory
- take vulnerable assets
- punish a rival
- stabilize internal structure
- recruit trusted people
- reduce pressure
- prepare for succession or leadership risk

## Police AI

Police should care about:
- visible crime
- public pressure
- political pressure
- investigations
- budget
- corruption risk
- repeat offenders
- major organizations
- sensitive districts

Possible priorities:
- respond to major incidents
- track known criminal assets
- protect important districts
- arrest high-value targets
- strengthen investigations
- reduce corruption exposure

## Business owner AI

Business owners should care about:
- profitability
- security
- survival
- regulation
- local pressure
- demand
- reputation

Possible priorities:
- raise prices
- close temporarily
- seek protection
- pay off threats
- relocate
- sell to a stronger actor
- become a front business

## Key NPC AI

Important NPCs should not only react.
They should push their own agendas.

They may:
- betray leaders
- seek promotion
- hoard resources
- form alliances
- protect family
- hide secrets
- switch loyalties
- attempt takeovers

## Priority modifiers

AI priorities should be changed by:
- fear
- greed
- loyalty
- ambition
- injury
- corruption
- pressure
- success
- failure
- social connections
- local city conditions

## Localized decision making

Actors should mostly care about the area they operate in.
They do not need perfect global awareness.

This makes the world feel more believable and also keeps the simulation more manageable.

## Reaction to the player

The AI should not exist only to oppose the player.
It should pursue its own priorities.
The player becomes a disruptive actor inside a moving system.

## Design principle

If an AI actor has no goals or priorities, it is probably too shallow.
If it has too many goals, it becomes unreadable.
The best model is a small number of strong priorities that create believable behavior.
