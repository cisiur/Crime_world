# Core Design Pillars

## 1. Real cities are the game board

The game is built on real-world cities and regions. OpenStreetMap geometry becomes the strategic map, while the simulation layer gives that map gameplay meaning.

## 2. The player starts as nobody

The player begins as a single character with no land, no power, and no formal organization. Growth must be earned.

## 3. The world simulates itself

NPCs, organizations, politics, police pressure, and the economy continue to evolve without direct player input.

## 4. The player plans, NPCs execute

The player is a strategist and organizer. Operations are planned by the player and carried out by NPCs.

## 5. Legal and illegal systems are intertwined

Legal businesses, influence, logistics, money laundering, and criminal operations all support one another.

## 6. Succession matters

Organizations can outlive the founder. Death, imprisonment, heirs, and internal power struggles are core gameplay events.

## 7. Emergent stories matter more than scripted missions

The game should generate memorable outcomes from interacting systems rather than from a long list of handcrafted missions.
