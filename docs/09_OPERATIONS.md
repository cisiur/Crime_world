# Operations System

## Purpose

Operations are the atomic actions of the game.
The player does not manually perform every act. The player plans operations and assigns NPCs to execute them.

## Operation structure

Every operation should define:
- target
- objective
- participants
- required assets
- risk level
- expected reward
- expected heat or pressure
- potential fallout

## Operation examples

- robbery
- car theft
- burglary
- extortion
- arson
- smuggling
- drug production
- drug distribution
- bribery
- blackmail
- infiltration
- sabotage
- recruitment
- laundering money
- intimidation
- assassination
- territory takeover

## Common operation properties

Different operation types can use the same framework with different parameters.

Example properties:
- planning time
- skill requirements
- number of participants
- stealth requirement
- violence requirement
- legal cover
- corruption support
- logistics support
- expected profit
- chance of failure

## Operation outcomes

Operations should have multiple possible results:
- clean success
- partial success
- success with exposure
- failure
- betrayal
- arrest
- retaliation
- escalation into open conflict

## Operation chains

One operation can trigger another.

Example:
- steal a car
- use the car for transport
- transport stolen goods
- launder the profits
- use the money to buy a front business

## Design goal

The system should reduce content bloat.
Instead of dozens of unrelated mechanics, the game should express many criminal actions through a flexible operation framework.
