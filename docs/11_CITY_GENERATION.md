# City Generation

## Goal

Turn real OpenStreetMap data into a playable strategic city.

## Input data

The generator starts from:
- roads
- buildings
- districts or administrative boundaries
- water
- railways
- ports
- airports
- landmarks
- public facilities
- commercial locations

## Output data

The generator produces:
- playable map geometry
- district values
- building metadata
- business categories
- strategic points
- hidden economy links
- criminal opportunity map
- police coverage map
- infrastructure value map

## Building classification

Buildings can be grouped into strategic classes such as:
- residential
- retail
- hospitality
- industrial
- logistics
- government
- police
- medical
- entertainment
- transport
- storage
- unknown

## District generation

Districts should be generated from a mix of real data and simulation rules.

Each district may receive hidden values like:
- wealth
- poverty
- corruption
- surveillance
- police strength
- criminal density
- business density
- political influence
- strategic value

## City personality generation

The same real city should still feel different from campaign to campaign because the simulation layer is generated anew.

The city personality should depend on:
- region
- country
- infrastructure
- economy
- density
- police quality
- corruption level
- global setting modifiers

## Strategic hotspots

The generator should identify valuable areas such as:
- central business districts
- transport hubs
- ports
- industrial zones
- border zones
- nightlife areas
- low-income neighborhoods
- affluent neighborhoods
- government clusters

## Design goal

The city should feel plausible, strategically readable, and different every run, while still remaining grounded in the real-world map.
