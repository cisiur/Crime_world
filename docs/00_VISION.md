# CrimeWorld Vision

## One-sentence pitch

CrimeWorld is a grand-strategy crime sandbox where the player starts as a single nobody in a real city and grows a criminal organization that can expand across cities, regions, and eventually entire countries.

## Core fantasy

The player does not play a mission-based criminal hero. The player builds a living criminal dynasty.

The game is about surviving, expanding, adapting, and eventually turning a local operation into a long-lasting empire that can outlive its founder.

## What makes the game unique

- Real-world cities are the map.
- OpenStreetMap geometry is transformed into a playable strategy layer.
- Legal and illegal businesses are deeply interconnected.
- NPCs behave like dynastic characters, not disposable units.
- Succession matters.
- The world continues to evolve even when the player is not directly acting.

## Player start fantasy

The player begins as one generated character with no power, no land, and no organization.

At the start the player has only:
- a personal profile,
- a small temporary camp or hideout,
- a few weak contacts,
- no territory,
- no formal authority.

The first phase of the game is about survival, early income, and building a small crew.

## Long-term fantasy

Over time the player can:
- recruit and train NPCs,
- establish safehouses and businesses,
- take control of districts,
- form alliances or rivalries with other groups,
- move into other cities,
- create cross-city networks,
- influence local politics and law enforcement,
- pass leadership to heirs or selected successors,
- survive leadership transitions, prison, civil war, and external pressure.

## Desired tone

The game is serious, systemic, and sandbox-driven, but not a realistic criminal training tool. It is fictional entertainment focused on strategy, power, and emergent stories.

## Design thesis

The most important part of the game is not the crime itself.

The most important part is the network of consequences created by crime, business, politics, relationships, succession, and territory.
