# MVP Content List

## Purpose

This document turns the MVP scope into a concrete content checklist.

## Playable city

The MVP uses one city only.
The city can be a placeholder or handcrafted city map.

## Buildings included in MVP

### Essential map entities
- residential buildings
- shops
- restaurants
- warehouses
- workshops
- transport company buildings
- police station
- basic medical facility
- a few strategic hideouts or safehouses
- a few district landmarks

### Optional if time allows
- clubs
- gas stations
- parking areas
- small offices
- storage yards

## Business types included in MVP

- street-level shop
- restaurant
- warehouse
- transport company
- workshop
- criminal hideout
- safehouse

## Operation types included in MVP

- robbery
- car theft
- extortion
- smuggling
- recruitment
- business takeover
- bribery

## NPC types included in MVP

- player character
- street recruit
- crew member
- gang rival
- police officer
- business owner
- informant
- simple lieutenant

## UI screens included in MVP

- city map
- organization panel
- NPC list
- businesses panel
- operations panel
- police pressure panel
- district overview
- save/load menu

## Systems included in MVP

- time control
- pause and speed modes
- simple reputation
- simple money flow
- simple police pressure
- basic territory or district control
- simple recruitment
- basic rival behavior
- save and load

## Systems postponed after MVP

- succession
- prison meta-game
- family trees
- multiple cities
- regional logistics
- global influence
- politics
- media systems
- international organizations
- advanced OSM world generation for every city

## Content rule

The MVP should be small enough to finish, but large enough to prove that the loop is fun.
