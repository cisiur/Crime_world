# City Economy Model

## Purpose

This document describes how a city economy works at the level needed for CrimeWorld.

The goal is not to simulate every transaction.
The goal is to simulate the relationships that matter for strategy, crime, pressure, and expansion.

## Core idea

A city economy is a network of demand, supply, labor, logistics, business activity, wealth distribution, and pressure.

The player and AI actors should be able to exploit gaps in that network.

## Main city economy inputs

A city economy can be influenced by:
- population
- income distribution
- employment
- transport quality
- district value
- business density
- security level
- corruption
- public fear
- crime levels
- available infrastructure

## Main city economy outputs

The economy should produce:
- demand for goods and services
- profits and losses
- job availability
- business growth or collapse
- property value changes
- logistics costs
- district attractiveness
- recruitment conditions
- criminal opportunities

## Economy as flows

The city economy should be understood as flows rather than isolated businesses.

Example flow:
population -> demand -> businesses -> jobs -> wages -> spending -> business growth

Example negative flow:
poor logistics -> higher costs -> weaker business margins -> closures -> unemployment -> crime growth

## Legal economy sectors

The legal economy can be grouped into sectors such as:
- retail
- hospitality
- transport
- construction
- real estate
- services
- industry
- finance
- media
- entertainment

## How economy affects gameplay

The player should be able to read the city economy and identify:
- which sectors are profitable,
- which sectors are weak,
- which districts are unstable,
- which routes are valuable,
- which businesses are vulnerable,
- where criminal pressure can be applied effectively.

## Business connection to the economy

Businesses should not be static income objects.
They should respond to economic conditions such as:
- demand
- rent
- security
- supply costs
- corruption
- local instability
- competition
- reputation

## Economic imbalance

Economic imbalance should create criminal openings.

Examples:
- a district with weak transport becomes good for smuggling,
- a wealthy district becomes good for high-value theft or extortion,
- a poor district becomes good for recruitment and low-level control,
- a business hub creates laundering and protection opportunities.

## Money in the economy

Money should not only appear as a reward.
It should move through the city in ways that matter to:
- businesses
- workers
- criminals
- police budgets
- corruption networks
- politics
- growth of districts

## Design principle

A city economy is good if it creates strategic tension.
If it only gives the player more or less money, it is too shallow.
