# MVP Balancing

## Balance goal

The game should feel difficult at the start, but not frustrating.

The player should feel underpowered, vulnerable, and uncertain at first, but every successful step should clearly matter.

## Core balance philosophy

- Early success must be earned.
- Failure should be possible, but not constant.
- Risk should create tension, not boredom.
- Growth should be visible after each meaningful win.
- The player should feel pressure rising as the organization grows.

## Starting state

The player begins with very limited resources.

Suggested starting state:
- one character
- one small hideout
- very little money
- no territory
- no strong allies
- no special protection

## Early economy

Early income should come from small, low-complexity operations.
These should provide enough reward to motivate the player, but not enough to trivialize progression.

## Operations balance rules

Every operation should have:
- a clear cost,
- a meaningful risk,
- a visible reward,
- a consequence when things go wrong.

A good early operation should not be guaranteed.
It should feel like a decision.

## Pressure balance rules

Pressure should rise when the player does loud, greedy, or repeated criminal actions.

Pressure must:
- grow fast enough to matter,
- but not so fast that the player is punished for experimenting.

## Rival balance rules

Rivals should be dangerous enough to block easy expansion.
They should pressure the player, take assets, retaliate, and make the city feel contested.

They should not be so strong that the player cannot recover from early mistakes.

## Reward pacing

The player should gain something tangible after each successful step:
- money
- access
- reputation
- contacts
- territory
- business control
- reduced risk in some area

## Difficulty curve

Suggested curve:

### Phase 1: survival
The player is weak and must choose carefully.

### Phase 2: foothold
The player builds a small crew and begins to stabilize.

### Phase 3: expansion
The player starts taking businesses and districts.

### Phase 4: pressure management
The player must balance growth with exposure.

### Phase 5: organization game
The challenge shifts from earning money to controlling a growing system.

## Anti-frustration rules

- The first few operations should teach the loop quickly.
- The player should not lose everything from one random failure.
- Recovery should always be possible.
- Important warnings should be readable before committing to risky actions.
- Consequences should be strong, but understandable.

## Tuning principle

If the player is never in danger, the game becomes shallow.
If the player is punished too hard, the game becomes annoying.
The goal is tension with recoverability.
